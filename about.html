About Us

Who we are
and why we compete.

Chilli Pepper Robotics is an elite, award-winning VEX organization dedicated to excellence in engineering, innovation, and competitive robotics.

Establishment

How it started

Chilli Pepper Robotics was founded by passionate students with a drive to compete at the highest levels of VEX Robotics. What started as a small group of builders and programmers has grown into a multi-city, award-winning organization spanning two countries.

From our very first tournament to provincial and national competitions, we've built a culture that prizes both competitive results and personal growth.

Location

Four cities, two countries

We operate across Ottawa and Markham, Ontario, Cary, North Carolina, and Tulsa, Oklahoma. Our teams train and compete locally while representing the Chilli Pepper name across North America.

This geographic spread is one of our strengths — different regional competitions, different opponents, and different styles all feed back into the organization.

Ottawa · Markham · Cary · Tulsa
Teams

Five teams, one banner

We field five competitive teams: 37X – Xtra Hot Chilli Peppers (Ottawa), 37S – Supa Hot Chilli Peppers (Ottawa), 2020Z – Double Take (Markham), 61568A – Genesis (Cary), and 3760V – The Unnamed (Tulsa).

Each team competes independently with its own identity, robot design, and strategy — while sharing knowledge, resources, and a collective drive to win.

Diversity

Not a school club — a community

Chilli Pepper Robotics isn't a single-school organization. Our members come from different schools, backgrounds, and cities across two countries. This diversity in perspective is one of our greatest competitive advantages — it fuels creative solutions and a richer team culture.

Multi-School · Multi-City
Mentorship

Everyone teaches. Everyone learns.

We pride ourselves on the mentorship, guidance, and advisory we provide one another — ranging from build and programming tips to engineering notebook help and match strategy.

Experienced members actively coach newer ones, creating a culture where the entire organization levels up together, not just individual teams.
